What this site is

Democracy Club Candidates is an open-source database of all candidates standing in UK elections. Its data powers Democracy Club's Who Can I Vote For?, and is regularly used by a wide range of organisations, including The Electoral Commission, social media companies, and national newspapers.

The core data collected by Democracy Club Candidates includes who is standing, what party theyâ€™re standing for, their contact details and social media accounts. The software requires that each change is submitted with a source, so that the collected information can be independently checked.

The database includes UK general elections from 2010, other scheduled elections from 2014/2015, and by-elections (including council by-elections) from May 2017. You can see a list of all elections in the database on the 'Numbers' page.

Please email {{ email }} with any feedback or bug reports. If you'd like to discuss this site or any other project relating to elections in the UK, we'd suggest that you join our Slack.

The site supports the following types of elections:

  • District, County, and Unitary councils (including the City of London Corporation and Isles of Scilly Council)
  • Directly elected mayors (Local Authority and Combined Authority)
  • Police and Crime Commissioners
  • London Assembly and Mayor of London
  • Northern Ireland Assembly
  • Scottish Parliament
  • Senedd Cymru/Welsh Parliament
  • UK Parliament

What this site is not

This site is a database of candidates only, and should not be regarded as an accurate list of current elected representatives. If an elected official leaves office or changes political party, this will not be recorded unless they subsequently stand for election under the new label. This also applies to the weblinks, statements, and other information attached to candidate profiles.

Making contributions

Anyone can sign up for an account and contribute to the database. Learn more about this on our Contributing page.

By signing up and contributing to this site, you agree to give copyright of the edit to Democracy Club. This doesn't apply to uploaded photos, but does apply to all other contributions.

We only ask this so that we are able to re-publish the information openly under an open licence such as CC-BY-SA or remove copyright restrictions by releasing into the public domain (CC0).

Disclaimer

The data on this website is crowd-sourced and may not be complete (or may contain inaccuracies). If you find an error, please either update the page yourself, or email us to report the problem.

Moderation and complaints

We operate a proactive moderation policy as regards images, and a reactive policy as regards text. All images uploaded to the site are checked by a staff member or trusted contributor before being published. Text published by users is regularly reviewed. Text about candidates contributed to this site is also published on WhoCanIVoteFor.co.uk, where we maintain feedback forms to allow readers to report issues to us.

We will respond to any and all complaints made about the service, unless obviously malicious. This includes complaint subjects as referred to by the Online Safety Act 2023:

  • Complaints about the content of the website.
  • Complaints about our management of the site.
  • Complaints about our moderation decisions, including where we have suspended a user.

Anyone can submit a complaint by emailing our support email.

Any text which we judge to be malicious or illegal in the UK will be removed as soon as it is brought to our attention, and the offending accounts will be banned from making further contributions. In particular, we would draw your attention to the list of offences provided in Schedules 5, 6 and 7 of the Online Safety Act 2023.

User accounts

User accounts created on this website do not require anything more than basic email verification. It is not possible to use these accounts for any purpose other than to edit election candidates; there is no functionality for communicating directly with other users. There is also no functionality for embellishing user profiles (for example, you cannot upload a photo of yourself, or add biographical information). Consequently, we believe the danger presented to individual users is extremely low.

Children

We do not require age verification to create an account. Based on past experience, we do not believe that this site offers any unique danger to child users, but we take this matter extremely seriously. Any attempt by users to ascertain age or biographical information about other users will likely result in an account ban.

As people below the age of 18 are ineligible to stand in UK elections, any attempt by a child to post information about themselves will be removed as soon as it is brought to our attention. However, the data produced by this site is intended for the use of all UK voters, including those aged 16 and 17; please bear this in mind when making edits.
